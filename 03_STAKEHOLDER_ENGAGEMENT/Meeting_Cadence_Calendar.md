# MediGlobal HealthTech Inc. | GRC Program

> **Document Owner:** GRC Program Director | **Last Updated:** Q1 2026 | **Classification:** INTERNAL — RESTRICTED
> **Company:** MediGlobal HealthTech Inc. (15,000 employees | 847 vendors | 42 hospitals | 156 clinics | 8 countries | 2.3M patients)

---

## PURPOSE

This document defines the complete meeting cadence for MediGlobal HealthTech Inc.'s GRC program. Every meeting is a critical governance touchpoint aligned to ISO 27001:2022 Annex A controls, HIPAA 45 CFR Part 164 requirements, and the company's risk management framework.

> **GRC Analyst Note:** Never skip these meetings — they are your primary mechanism for staying ahead of compliance gaps, catching vendor issues early, and maintaining executive visibility. Missing even one quarterly board meeting can result in delayed budget approval for critical security controls.

---

## MEETING HIERARCHY OVERVIEW

```
LEVEL 1: Board Audit Committee (Quarterly)     ─── Strategic Governance
LEVEL 2: Executive Steering Committee (Monthly) ─── Executive Oversight
LEVEL 3: GRC Steering Committee (Bi-weekly)     ─── Program Governance
LEVEL 4: Departmental Risk Meetings (Monthly)   ─── Operational Risk
LEVEL 5: Vendor Review Meetings (Monthly)       ─── Third-Party Risk
LEVEL 6: Incident Response Sync (Weekly)        ─── Active Incidents
LEVEL 7: Daily Standup (Daily)                  ─── Team Operations
```

## LEVEL 1: BOARD AUDIT COMMITTEE — QUARTERLY MEETING

### Schedule
- **Frequency:** Quarterly | Q1=March | Q2=June | Q3=September | Q4=December
- **Duration:** 2 hours
- **Location:** MediGlobal Global HQ, Chicago — Boardroom 1A (or secure Teams)

### Required Attendees
- Board Audit Committee (5 members)
- CEO — Dr. Margaret Chen | CISO — James Nakamura | CCO — Sarah Patel
- CRO — Robert Okonkwo | CMIO — Dr. Amara Diallo
- External Auditor — Deloitte | Legal Counsel — Winston & Strawn LLP

### Pre-Meeting Preparation (GRC Analyst Responsibilities)
- [ ] Compile quarterly risk register update (top 20 risks with financial impact)
- [ ] Prepare ISO 27001 compliance scorecard (% controls implemented per domain)
- [ ] Draft HIPAA incident summary (breaches, near-misses, OCR communications)
- [ ] Update vendor risk dashboard (high-risk vendors, BAA status for all 847 vendors)
- [ ] Prepare financial impact analysis for open/accepted risks
- [ ] Draft 2-page executive summary — board will NOT read 50-page reports
- [ ] Distribute board package 10 business days in advance
- [ ] Confirm quorum (minimum 3 board members required for decisions)

### Agenda Template (120 minutes)

| Time | Item | Owner | Duration |
|------|------|-------|----------|
| 0:00 | Call to order + approval of previous minutes | Board Chair | 5 min |
| 0:05 | CISO Security Program Update | CISO | 20 min |
| 0:25 | ISO 27001 + HIPAA Compliance Status | CCO | 20 min |
| 0:45 | Top Risk Briefing — Q Highlights | CRO | 15 min |
| 1:00 | Vendor & Third-Party Risk Summary | GRC Director | 10 min |
| 1:10 | Incident Review — Material Events | CISO/Legal | 10 min |
| 1:20 | Budget Review — Security & Compliance | CFO | 10 min |
| 1:30 | Regulatory Update — New Requirements | CCO/Legal | 10 min |
| 1:40 | Open Discussion & Board Questions | All | 15 min |
| 1:55 | Action items review & adjournment | Board Chair | 5 min |

### ISO 27001 Controls Addressed
- **A.5.1** — Information security policies (board approval required)
- **A.6.1** — Internal organization (roles and responsibilities confirmed)
- **A.18.2** — Compliance reviews
- **A.5.36** — Compliance with policies, rules and standards

### HIPAA Requirements Addressed
- 45 CFR 164.308(a)(1) — Security management process
- 45 CFR 164.308(a)(8) — Evaluation
- 45 CFR 164.530(j) — Documentation requirements

### Real-World GRC Analyst Challenges at This Meeting
1. **Board lacks technical context** — Translate technical risks into financial/legal impact. "847 vendors with incomplete BAAs, representing $340M in vendor spend" hits harder than "47% BAA completion rate."
2. **Multi-jurisdictional conflicts** — 8 countries (US, UK, Germany, France, India, Australia, Brazil, Singapore) mean EU GDPR, UK DPA, HIPAA, and local laws have conflicting deadlines. Prepare ONE consolidated regulatory calendar.
3. **Budget justification pressure** — Board questions the $8.5M security budget. Always tie dollars to risk reduction: "MFA implementation ($240K cost) reduced phishing success rate from 4.2% to 0.3%, preventing estimated $2.1M in breach costs."
4. **Audit fatigue** — Board members receive 12+ audit reports annually. Make your report stand out with visual dashboards, not walls of text.
5. **Conflicting risk appetites** — Clinical leadership wants workflow efficiency, Security wants strict controls. Frame compliance as protecting the company's $9.2B revenue, not just as a checkbox exercise.

## LEVEL 5: INCIDENT RESPONSE SYNC — WEEKLY

### Schedule
- **Normal:** Every Wednesday, 9:00 AM – 9:30 AM CST
- **During P1/P2 Incidents:** Daily at 8:00 AM (until incident resolved)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes normal | Up to 60 minutes during active incidents

### Required Attendees
- CISO James Nakamura (or delegate)
- SOC Manager
- GRC Program Director
- Incident Response Lead
- Privacy Officer (if PHI potentially involved)
- General Counsel/Legal (if breach notification may be triggered)

### Agenda (30 minutes)
1. Active incident status — all P1 and P2 incidents, current containment status (10 min)
2. New threats/vulnerabilities requiring immediate attention (5 min)
3. Recently closed incidents — brief lessons learned summary (5 min)
4. Regulatory notification deadline tracking — HIPAA 60-day, GDPR 72-hour clocks (5 min)
5. Resource needs and escalation decisions (5 min)

### GRC Analyst Responsibilities During Active Incidents
- Open ServiceNow IRM incident ticket within 30 minutes of P1/P2 confirmation
- Start HIPAA breach notification 60-day clock in ServiceNow (if PHI involved)
- Coordinate attorney-client privilege protections with Legal before external investigation begins
- Maintain evidence chain of custody log — every person who touches evidence must be logged
- If >500 individuals affected: prepare OCR (Office for Civil Rights) notification draft
- Update risk register with new risk entry after incident is contained
- Conduct lessons learned session within 5 business days of incident close

### Incident Severity Definitions at MediGlobal
| Priority | Definition | Response Time | Examples |
|----------|-----------|---------------|---------|
| P1 Critical | Confirmed breach or system outage >4 hours | Immediate (24/7) | Active ransomware, PHI exfiltration, EHR system down |
| P2 High | Potential breach or system degradation | 4 hours | Unauthorized access attempt, vendor breach notification, suspicious data transfer |
| P3 Medium | Policy violation or non-critical security event | 24 hours | Failed MFA bypass attempt, policy exception request, phishing click |
| P4 Low | Informational security event | 72 hours | Firewall block notification, security awareness failure, minor policy violation |

## LEVEL 6: GRC TEAM DAILY STANDUP — 15-MINUTE DAILY SYNC

### Schedule
- **Frequency:** Monday–Friday, 9:00 AM – 9:15 AM CST (strict 15 minutes)
- **Format:** Microsoft Teams video call
- **No slide decks** — verbal updates only with shared screen on task tracker

### Attendees
- GRC Program Director (or Senior Analyst if Director unavailable)
- All GRC Analysts (Levels I–III)
- Risk Analysts
- Compliance Analysts

### Standup Format — 3 Questions Per Person
1. **What did I complete yesterday?** (specific deliverable or milestone)
2. **What am I working on today?** (specific task with estimated completion)
3. **Any blockers or escalations needed?** (yes/no — detail discussed after standup)

### Common Standup Topics
- ISO 27001 control testing progress (are we on track for next audit?)
- Evidence collection status (what % complete for upcoming audit window?)
- Vendor assessment queue (how many vendors in queue? any overdue?)
- Policy review deadlines approaching in next 14 days
- Regulatory filing deadlines
- Open incidents requiring GRC support

### Tips for Effective Standups
- Keep to 15 minutes strictly — parking lot non-critical discussions
- Use ServiceNow task numbers when referencing work items
- If someone is blocked, assign a follow-up meeting immediately after standup
- GRC Director should note patterns: if the same person is blocked repeatedly, there's a systemic issue

## MEETING EFFECTIVENESS BEST PRACTICES — GRC ANALYST GUIDE

### Before Every Meeting
1. **Distribute pre-read materials 3+ days in advance** — Executives will not read 50-page reports during the meeting. They read in advance or not at all.
2. **Prepare 3 key messages** — What do you need from this group? Decision/Approval/Awareness/Escalation? Know before you walk in.
3. **Know your numbers cold** — 847 vendors, $2.1M average breach cost, 98.3% training completion, $8.5M budget, 93 ISO controls. Be ready to cite any figure without looking.
4. **Anticipate challenges** — What will the CFO, CISO, or board member likely challenge? Prepare responses.
5. **Identify escalation items** — What needs a decision TODAY vs. can wait until next meeting?

### During Every Meeting
1. **Take verbatim action items** — Who, what, and by when. "Someone will look into that" is NOT an action item.
2. **Document dissenting opinions** — If a board member objects to a risk acceptance, document it — it protects you and the company.
3. **Use a parking lot** — Off-topic but important items go to parking lot, not the trash. Address in next meeting or async.
4. **Call time at T-5 minutes** — Review action items before adjourning, don't let the meeting end without clear next steps.

### After Every Meeting
1. **Send meeting minutes within 24 hours** — Not 48, not Friday afternoon. 24 hours. This is non-negotiable at MediGlobal.
2. **Create ServiceNow tickets for every action item** — Assign owner, due date, and link to risk or control in the risk register.
3. **Update the risk register immediately** — Any new risks, changed risk ratings, or new controls discussed in the meeting must be reflected within 24 hours.
4. **Distribute to stakeholders who couldn't attend** — Include key decisions, not just action items.

### Common Meeting Failure Patterns to Avoid
| Failure Pattern | Real-World Impact | Prevention |
|----------------|-------------------|-----------|
| No agenda distributed | Executives come unprepared, no decisions made | Send agenda 3 days prior with pre-read |
| Action items with no owners | Items never completed, issues escalate | Name a specific person for every action |
| Wrong attendees in room | Decision-makers absent, need "another meeting" | Pre-confirm attendees 48 hours before |
| Missing metrics | "We think we're compliant" sounds hollow | Always include specific percentages and counts |
| No escalation path | Issues fester at operational level | Every meeting should have an explicit escalation decision point |
| Showing up to a board meeting with bad news they haven't heard | Career-limiting surprise | Pre-brief key stakeholders 48 hours before board meetings |

## ESCALATION MATRIX

| Issue Type | First Escalation | Second Escalation | Board-Level Trigger |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Active data breach | CISO within 1 hour | CEO within 4 hours | Within 24 hours if >500 patients affected |
| Critical vendor failure (e.g., Cerner/Epic breach) | Vendor Mgr → CISO | CCO within 24 hours | If patient care impacted or revenue impact >$1M |
| Regulatory citation/investigation | CCO immediately | CEO + General Counsel | If fine potential >$500K or OCR investigation opened |
| Failed ISO 27001 surveillance audit | GRC Director → CCO | CISO within 24 hours | Board at next quarterly with remediation plan |
| Budget overrun >$100K | GRC Director → CFO | CEO approval required | Board if overrun >$500K |
| Critical system outage >4 hours | IT Director → CISO | CEO if patient safety risk | Board if patient safety actually impacted |
| Insider threat confirmed | HR + Legal + CISO simultaneously | CEO within 2 hours | Board within 48 hours if executive involved |

## REGULATORY NOTIFICATION DEADLINES

| Regulation | Trigger | Deadline | Recipient | Responsible Party |
|-----------|---------|----------|-----------|------------------|
| HIPAA Breach >500 individuals | Confirmed PHI breach | 60 calendar days from discovery | HHS OCR via online portal | CCO + Privacy Officer |
| HIPAA Breach <500 individuals | Confirmed PHI breach | March 1 of following year | HHS OCR annual log | Privacy Officer |
| GDPR Breach | Personal data breach (EU individuals) | 72 hours from awareness | Relevant EU Data Protection Authority | EU Privacy Officer (EMEA) |
| UK DPA Breach | Personal data breach (UK individuals) | 72 hours from awareness | UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) | UK Privacy Officer |
| State breach laws (varies by state) | Varies by state definition | 30–90 days (state-specific) | State Attorney General + affected individuals | Legal + Compliance |
| SOX material weakness | Internal control failure | Immediate upon discovery | Board Audit Committee + SEC (via 8-K) | CFO + General Counsel |
